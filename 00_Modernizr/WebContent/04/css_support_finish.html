Bryce Canyon

Bryce Canyon National Park in Southern Utah is a natural amphitheater with distinctive pillars called hoodoos caused by wind, water, and ice erosion.

Individual hoodoos reach up to 200 foot (60m) in height. In summer, the heat on the floor of the canyon can be oppressive. But in winter, the temperature regularly falls below freezing.
